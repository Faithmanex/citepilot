AI uncited factual claims

Factual, empirical, or statistical assertions in the body text that lack a citation marker.

"Approximately 68% of graduate students report difficulty locating primary sources."
PARAGRAPH 12PAGE 6STATISTICAL CLAIM
This is a specific statistic presented as established fact. Without a citation, a reader has no way to verify the figure or trace it to its source study.
"This approach has become the dominant framework in the field since 2015."
PARAGRAPH 18PAGE 8TREND CLAIM
Claims about a field-wide shift or consensus require a supporting citation — otherwise the assertion reads as the author's opinion rather than a documented trend.
"Early intervention reduces relapse rates by nearly half."
PARAGRAPH 24PAGE 11EMPIRICAL CLAIM
A causal, quantified outcome like this needs a direct citation to the study it's drawn from.
